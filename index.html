Who am I?

I am currently a sophomore at the University of Minnesota Twin Cities pursuring a degree in electrical engineering and a minor in computer science.

Outside of the classroom, I am involved in various student engineering groups as well as an active member of the computer science and hacking community of the wider Twin Cities area.

I am the electrical subteam lead of the Advanced Aerostat Project, a student group I helped create my freshman year in which we are pursuing to break the duration record for the longest flight of a latex weather balloon by designing, building, and optimizing an autonomous left regulating system. Another groups I am very active in is the universities chemical engineering car. As the sole electrical engineer on the team, my responsibilities mainly revolve around the electrical dive and control systems that interface and regulate the chemical processes powering the car. We compete with teams from other universities from across the nation and the world. If you would like to see more details about my involvement in these groups, see their sections above.
